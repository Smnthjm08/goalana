## Live-devnet settlement/claims validation (2026-07-16)

Ran `settleFinishedFixtures()` for real against the two fixtures that had actually finished (France v Spain, England v Argentina — real World Cup 2026 matches, real TxLINE `finalSeq`/stat data). Found a real bug, confirmed a prior fix, and fully validated the claims path:

- **Found:** all 9 markets on those two fixtures failed on-chain with `StaleOracleSnapshot`. Root cause: `settleAfter` is baked immutably into the Market account at creation time. These 9 were created with an old buffer (~kickoff+3h); real match-finalization timestamps land ~2h post-kickoff, so the on-chain check (`oracle_ts_secs >= market.settle_after`) could never pass. `market.service.ts:288-303` shows this exact issue was already diagnosed and fixed for markets created *going forward* (now `locksAt + 15min`), but the fix isn't retroactive — settle_after can't be changed after creation.
- **Resolved:** cancelled all 9 stuck markets on-chain (`cancel_market`, authority-gated, unconditional) — they had $0 in either pool, so no funds were at risk. DB synced to `CANCELLED`. They would otherwise have sat at `LOCKED` forever.
- **Claims fully validated end-to-end on real devnet:** since neither upcoming semifinal (France v England, Spain v Argentina — both ~2-3 days out at the time) had TxLINE-priced full-time odds yet, created one on-chain market with a synthetic (never-true) predicate tied to a real fixture, then ran `create_market` → `place_bet` (0.02 SOL) → `cancel_market` → `claim_refund` — all four real transactions succeeded. Confirms the full claim path works; DB row for the synthetic market was deleted afterward so it doesn't show in the real fixture's market list.
- **Not yet validated:** `claim_winnings` specifically (the win-payout math path) — only `claim_refund` was exercised live. Needs a market that actually settles (not cancels) with a real bet on the winning side; blocked on TxLINE pricing the upcoming semifinals' full-time markets, or a later real fixture.

## 2026-07-19 — Half-time markets: investigated, deferred

Prompted by a request to add half-time win markets to `SUPPORTED_MARKETS`. Checked real data before writing any code, twice: once against `docs/odds.xlsx` (a sample export), then again against `odds-current-db.xlsx` (a same-day production dump of the live `Odds` table) for fixture `18257865` (France v England semifinal).

**Confirmed real half-time odds exist and are actively priced.** Both exports show genuine rows with `marketPeriod = "half=1"`:

- `1X2_PARTICIPANT_RESULT` / `marketParameters=""` / `marketPeriod="half=1"` — home/draw/away prices present (production dump: `[2423,2726,4535]` → `["41.271","36.684","22.051"]`)
- `OVERUNDER_PARTICIPANT_GOALS` at lines 0.5 through 2.5, all with `marketPeriod="half=1"` and live prices
- `ASIANHANDICAP_PARTICIPANT_GOALS` also has `half=1` rows, though nothing in `SUPPORTED_MARKETS` uses that `superOddsType` today

**Confirmed why no market gets created for any of them.** `discoverMarketsForFixture` (`apps/api/src/services/market.service.ts:110-230`) groups odds rows by the key `${superOddsType}|${marketPeriod}|${marketParameters}` (`:122`) and only ever matches against `SUPPORTED_MARKETS`/`OVER_UNDER_MARKETS` entries, every one of which hardcodes `marketPeriod: ""` in `apps/api/src/services/market-definitions.ts`. A `half=1` row therefore never matches — not rejected, just never looked at. This is exactly what `market.service.spec.ts:128-152` already documents: a `half=1` 1X2 row explicitly commented `// First half not supported yet`, asserted to discover 0 markets.

**Pricing/discovery is easy; settlement is the real blocker.** Adding `HALF_TIME_HOME_WIN`/`DRAW`/`AWAY_WIN` (+ over/under at the lines confirmed above) to `market-definitions.ts` with `marketPeriod: "half=1"`, and extending the matching loops + `computeCurrentReferenceProbability`'s switch (`market.service.ts:80-93`), would light up real discovery immediately. But the on-chain `Predicate` (`packages/goalana-sdk/src/predicate.ts`) has no period field — it proves `HOME_GOALS`/`AWAY_GOALS` via a Merkle proof fetched through `getScoresStatValidation({fixtureId, seq, statKey, statKey2})` (`packages/txline/src/services/scores-service.ts:139`), which has **no period parameter at all**. Per `packages/goalana-sdk/src/txline-stats.ts:49-51`'s own comment, every real proof fetched so far comes back as `period=100` (full match); non-full-match periods are explicitly flagged "unverified against `settle_market`" — the same reason extra-time/penalty markets were never added. A half-time market created today would price correctly at creation but settle against the *full-match* goal count, not the half-time score — silently wrong outcomes, not just untested.

**Plan (deferred, not implemented this pass):**

1. Probe TxLINE's real `/scores/stat-validation` endpoint to see whether it can even return a `period=1` proof on request (undocumented either way in this repo — needs a live check, separate from this item).
2. If it can: add a `period` param to `getScoresStatValidation`, thread it through `settlement.service.ts`'s two call sites (`:140`, `:236`) and `SettleMarketParams.statA.statToProve.period`. Note this also surfaces a latent gap in existing full-time markets: nothing today pins a market to a specific period at creation time — it's implicit in whatever period the proof-fetch happens to return.
3. Only then add `HALF_TIME_*` to `SUPPORTED_MARKETS` with `supportedForCreation: true`.
4. If demo-visible discovery is wanted sooner without the settlement risk, add the market defs with `supportedForCreation: false` (mirrors how unsupported/malformed markets are already surfaced) so the UI can show "half-time market detected, not yet tradeable" without ever risking a mis-settled real-money market.
